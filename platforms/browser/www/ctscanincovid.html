IS CT-SCAN NECESSARY FOR COVID PATIENTS?

Many people today are puzzled whether if CT-SCAN is required for checking if you are affected by COVID and what is its use?. So today we will dig deeper into this and will try to find if it is backed by any scientific evidence.

LET'S SEE WHAT IS A THROAT SWAB TEST

So if anyone shows any symptoms of COVID then firstly the throat swab test is done to do the rapid screening. In this a sample is taken by using the swab and sent for the test, again there are two tests one being the rapid test which takes about 30 minutes, and the other one being the RT-PCR test which is a routine test and takes about 2-3 days. However, the sensitivity obtained by these tests aren't always correct.

LET'S SEE WHAT STUDIES SAY

Earlier in China, it was found that the RT-PCR test was 30-70% accurate and CT-SCANS had much better sensitivity but later data collected from the United States of America shows that RT-PCR tests were 95% sensitive and the use of CT-SCAN was negligible due to this fact.

LET'S SEE WHY IS X-RAY SCAN REQUIRED?

Those patients who show the symptoms related to lungs or difficulty in breathing are consulted to take an X-RAY. In X-RAY we get three types of results that are PATCHY(GROUND GLASS OPACITIES) which looks like frosted glass and show the alveoli which is a balloon-like sack in LUNGS responsible for Oxygen diffusion which gets filled with a fluid slowly turning grey in the scan or more severe infections more and more fluid will accumulate in lungs causing SOLID WHITE CONSOLIDATION or even a condition called as CRAZY PAVING PATTERN which occurs due to swelling of Alveoli within the interstitial space.

LET'S SEE HOW THEY OCCUR

If anyone has a consolidation then that person might suffer a serious infection. Most of the time GROUND GLASS, SOLID WHITE CONSOLIDATION, and CRAZY PAVING PATTERN can be found in isolation or combination. Generally, GROUND GLASS is the first sign followed by another or both. These commonly affect multiple lobes or can even be isolated to one lobe, can be found in both lungs, and are found near the periphery.

LET'S SEE WHY IS CT-SCAN REQUIRED IN SOME PATIENTS?

sometimes CT-SCAN is required but for a very negligible number of patients because if somebody is either seriously infected or if somebody's covid test turns out to be negative even though that person shows the symptoms only then CT-SCAN is done. Even sometimes the patients who are treated don't show satisfactory recovery and their health declines. At such times the use of CT-SCAN is done if that person has any severe problem. Mostly if anyone has any problem then it is detected in the X-RAY but at times if X-RAY doesn't provide much information causing the dilemma only then the CT-SCAN is done. Whenever some patients suffer from severe infections it is observed that their CT-SCANS reveal blood clots in their lungs a condition knows as pulmonary embolism or clear scans of GROUND GLASS, SOLID WHITE CONSOLIDATIONS, and CRAZY PAVING PATTERN.

REASON FOR NOT USING IT ON EVERY PATIENT

The reasons why only a negligible number of people are consulted for CT-SACN are because:-

1. TRANSMISSION RISK

Firstly if anyone is a positive patient then making them go for a CT-SCAN can cause the transmission to others involved in this process and even to other patients who visit for CT-SCAN.

2. SLOW PROCESS

Secondly whenever COVID infected patient takes a CT-SCAN the machine and nearby area is sanitized to maintain hygiene which takes time so other serious patients may have to wait until sanitization is done.

3. RADIATION

The third reason is that CT-SCAN and X-RAY cause exposure to radiation which is not good for the body and should be only done in serious cases.

4. EXPENSIVE

The fourth but final reason is that CT-SCAN is an expensive procedure and takes time. So most people can't afford to spend or to wait in such a time of crisis.

5. UNETHICAL PRACTICE

Lastly, there is another point which I would like to add to this is that consulting CT-SCAN to everyone even when it is not necessary would be against the medical ethics and would even spread the wrong message among the general public.
